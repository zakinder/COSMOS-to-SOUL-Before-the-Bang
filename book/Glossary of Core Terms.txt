Glossary of Core Terms
This glossary defines the core terms used throughout COSMOS to SOUL: Before the Bang. These definitions are not intended as dictionary meanings alone, but as working concepts within the manuscript's larger framework of cosmology, motion, consciousness, truth, and acknowledgment.
ACK — Acknowledgment as a living principle of recognition, response, and relational continuity. In the framework of this book, ACK is not merely agreement, but the act of consciously registering existence, truth, or presence.
Absolute Nothingness — A state prior to matter, light, time, structure, and observation. It is presented as a condition beyond darkness and beyond ordinary concepts of emptiness.
Approx Truth — A near-truth or workable truth that functions within limited perception, incomplete knowledge, or living uncertainty. It is contrasted with more exact or structural forms of truth.
Continuum — The ongoing extension of existence beyond isolated moments. In this manuscript, it refers to the unbroken movement linking origin, creation, consciousness, acknowledgment, and return.
COSMOS to SOUL — The central framework of the book, expressing the progression from universal origin and material structure toward consciousness, inner witnessing, and spiritual acknowledgment.
Delta — The first disturbance or shift in absolute nothingness. It signifies difference, asymmetry, and the smallest change that makes all later creation possible.
False Types — Categories of distortion, misalignment, or imitation that appear in contrast to genuine truth. These types help define what departs from continuity, accuracy, or alignment.
Infinite ACK Gate — A culminating state of alignment in which acknowledgment becomes continuous, unbroken, and fully integrated with the larger framework of existence.
Infinite Continuum — The extension of being beyond linear sequence into a state of ongoing connection, return, and continuity without final interruption.
Motion — The signature of existence. In the book's framework, motion includes physical movement, inner change, inquiry, transformation, recovery, and the evidence that reality is active rather than static.
Motion Debt — Unresolved movement within the self or a system. It refers to unfinished cycles, unintegrated truth, or consequences that remain active until acknowledged or completed.
Observer — A witnessing presence capable of recognizing pattern, truth, or existence. In later sections, the observer becomes closely related to consciousness and soul.
Override — The conscious interruption or redirection of destructive, repetitive, or misaligned patterns. It is associated with agency, discipline, and recovery.
Pair State — A relational condition in which two presences, systems, or forms of awareness enter mutual acknowledgment, alignment, or shared continuity.
RealTime — Lived, direct, unfolding experience as it is encountered in natural sequence. It is contrasted with compressed, artificial, or mediated temporal experience.
Respect — A foundational form of acknowledgment that recognizes the existence, integrity, and reality of another being, system, truth, or process.
SA611982 Seal — The symbolic marker of continuity, witness, authorship, and return that appears throughout the manuscript to signify conceptual completion and reconnection to source.
Soul — The inward witness or conscious depth through which existence becomes personally known, remembered, and acknowledged. In this work, the soul is linked to observation, memory, and alignment.
Style — The pattern through which identity becomes visible. Style is presented as the distinctive signature of motion, structure, behavior, or expression across scales of existence.
Truth — That which remains aligned with reality, continuity, and recognizable pattern. In this manuscript, truth is often connected to motion, structure, endurance, and acknowledgment.
Truth Types — The structured categories through which truth is identified, described, and differentiated within the conceptual system of the book.
Void — The pre-creative condition of absolute nothingness before matter, time, light, or structure. It is the opening field from which the first disturbance emerges.
ZoomTime — A mediated, accelerated, compressed, or artificial experience of time that differs from the direct unfolding quality of RealTime.
