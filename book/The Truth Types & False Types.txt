The Truth Types & False Types
This section outlines the manuscript's primary distinction between forms of truth and forms of falseness. These categories are not offered merely as abstract labels, but as working patterns that help the reader interpret motion, structure, intention, continuity, distortion, and alignment throughout COSMOS to SOUL: Before the Bang.
Truth Types
1.	Genuine Truth — That which remains aligned with reality whether or not it is convenient, desired, or immediately understood.
2.	Motion Truth — Truth revealed through change, continuity, movement, and what remains active across time.
3.	Precision Truth — Truth expressed through exactness, structure, accurate relation, and disciplined clarity.
4.	Approx Truth — A partial or near truth that functions within limited perception, incomplete knowledge, or developing understanding.
5.	Pattern Truth — Truth visible through repetition, structure, recurrence, and the consistency of form across scales.
6.	Witness Truth — Truth confirmed through direct observation, inward recognition, or conscious acknowledgment.
7.	Lived Truth — Truth tested and revealed through experience, endurance, consequence, and personal transformation.
8.	Relational Truth — Truth that becomes visible through alignment, reciprocity, respect, and acknowledged connection between beings or systems.
False Types
1.	Imitation False — A constructed appearance that resembles truth outwardly while lacking its inner continuity or substance.
2.	Broken Truth — A truth fragment removed from its larger context and therefore distorted in meaning or use.
3.	Override False — A falseness maintained through force, suppression, or repetitive interruption of what would otherwise become visible.
4.	Convenience False — A preferred untruth accepted because it is easier, safer, or less demanding than reality.
5.	Pattern False — A repeated distortion that gains credibility through familiarity rather than alignment.
6.	Performance False — A false presentation sustained for appearance, status, persuasion, or concealment rather than truth.
7.	Borrowed False — A falsehood inherited, repeated, or adopted without direct examination or lived verification.
8.	Collapse False — A form of falseness born from confusion, fragmentation, exhaustion, or unresolved contradiction.
In the framework of this book, truth is not defined only by statement, but by endurance, coherence, motion, and acknowledgment. Falsehood is not defined only by error, but by distortion, misalignment, interruption, and separation from living continuity. These distinctions prepare the reader to recognize how the manuscript interprets creation, consciousness, human struggle, and the search for what remains real.
